Summarizing a plug-in’s life cycle

The following steps describe the life cycle of a plug-in:

1.At startup, Acrobat Reader or Acrobat searches its plug-in directory for plug-in files.

2.For each plug-in file, Acrobat Reader or Acrobat attempts to load the file. If the plug-in is successfully loaded, Acrobat Reader or Acrobat invokes routines in PIMain.c that complete the handshaking process.

3.Acrobat Reader or Acrobat invokes callback functions in this order:

  • PluginExportHFTs 

  • PluginImportReplaceAndRegister 

  • PluginInit 

This sequence establishes the linkages between the plug-in and Acrobat or Acrobat Reader, and between the plug-in and any other plug-ins. After all plug-ins are loaded, Acrobat or Acrobat Reader continues its own loading and starts the user interface. It adds plug-in tools to the toolbar, and plug-in menu items to the menu bar. Then it starts the user session.
